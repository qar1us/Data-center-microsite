02 For Residents

Your first three moves.

01

Get the facts

Opacity is widespread, but most state laws let you request records — including non-disclosure agreements (NDAs) and contracts. Ask a local civil society organization for a cost-benefit analysis of what the project means for your rates.

02

Show up in numbers

Pack the hearing and circulate a petition — a large presence and thousands of signatures give officials a reason to act. Demand a breakdown of contract terms and tax exemptions before any vote.

03

Use your power

Intervene at the Public Utility Commission, place a transparency ordinance on the ballot, vote for the commissioners who set your rates, or recall officials who won’t listen.

03 Proof

It’s already working.

Communities are not powerless — these are real outcomes, driven by residents and local officials who organized and showed up.

Port Washington, WI

Residents formed a nonprofit, gathered ~1,000 signatures, and passed a ballot measure that now requires voter approval before the city grants data center tax incentives.

Cascade Locks, OR

Voters recalled two officials who backed a 10 MW data center, and two other project advocates lost their re-election bids.

Pima County, AZ

Public backlash over Project Blue NDAs forced a 90-day sunshine period, a two-year cap on economic-development NDA terms, and quarterly disclosure.

Minnesota

CURE MN intervened before the Public Utility Commission, which then rejected Amazon’s request to skip a full regulatory review.
